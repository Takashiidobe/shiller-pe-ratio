Shiller P/E Ratio

The shiller P/E Ratio is a price earnings ratio based on average inflation-adjusted earnings from the previous 10 years, also known as the Cyclically Adjusted P/E Ratio (CAPE)

This chart shows whether or not the market is overvalued -- since 1960, the average P/E Ratio is about 21, but currently the market is trading at about 38, which is 2 standard deviations over the mean. The highest ever Shiller P/E was 44, at the height of the dot-com bubble.
